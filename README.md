👩‍💼 HR Analytics SQL Project

📌 Overview

This project focuses on analyzing employee data using SQL to uncover insights related to attrition, salary distribution, employee performance, and workforce trends.

The goal is to simulate how HR teams can use data to improve employee retention, satisfaction, and organizational decision-making.

🎯 Objectives

Analyze employee attrition patterns
Identify high-risk employee segments
Understand salary and promotion trends
Evaluate work-life balance and job satisfaction
Compare department-level performance metrics

📂 Dataset

The dataset contains employee-level information including:

Demographics (Age, Gender, Marital Status)
Job details (Role, Department, Job Level)
Compensation (Monthly Income, Salary Hike)
Work metrics (Years at Company, Promotions)
Satisfaction scores (Environment, Relationship, Work-Life Balance)

🧠 Key SQL Concepts Used

✔ Aggregations (SUM, AVG, COUNT)
✔ CASE statements
✔ Subqueries
✔ Common Table Expressions (CTEs)
✔ Window Functions (RANK, ROW_NUMBER)
✔ Conditional analysis
✔ Grouping & filtering

🔍 Business Questions Solved

📊 Employee & Workforce Insights
  Top 5 longest-tenured employees
  Average tenure by department
  Promotion frequency across job roles

💰 Compensation Analysis
  Average salary per job role
  Salary distribution by education
  Employees earning above department average

📉 Attrition Analysis
  Attrition rate by department
  Attrition by age group
  Overtime vs attrition impact
  Relationship satisfaction vs attrition

🧩 Advanced Insights
  Most popular departments by work-life balance
  Top earners ranked within departments
  Attrition vs company average comparison
  Employees with multiple promotions

📈 Key Insights You Can Derive
  Departments with high employee turnover
  Impact of overtime on attrition
  Relationship between salary and retention
  Role of work-life balance in employee satisfaction
  Identification of top-performing and high-value employees
